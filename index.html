Hi, I am Aref!

I am a graduate master's student of Computer Science at Shahid Beheshti University, Tehran. In my master's, I worked on Spiking Neural Network, and my master's thesis was titled: "Gesture Recognition via Spike-Convolutional Neural Networks (S-CNN)." In short, Spiking Neural Networks are a very new branch of Neural Networks, which has more similarity to the human brain by mimicking the brain's electrical pulse mechanism. In other words, neurons in SNN generate impulses rather than continuous values in conventional networks. I used this type of network to solve human gesture recognition tasks. Please find my thesis and correspondent project on my GitHub at ArefMq/action-recognition-via-snn.

My interest in topics of computer vision stems from my under-graduation era. I had joined NAO-Biped Laboratory as I began my bachelor's degree. In NAO-Lab, I start working on computer vision and image processing tasks such as "Soccer Field Object Recognition" or image segmentation and analysis in general. These tasks aimed to enable robots to play soccer in an artificial environment and participate in RoboCup SPL Champions.

More recently, however, I have switched to Software Engineering in more enterprised projects. As a matter of fact, until last year, I have been working at Cafe Bazaar, one of Iran's biggest software firms, as a Software and Data Engineer. In this position, I start working in the Tech-Lead role, and later on, I have switched to try Team-Leader role.
